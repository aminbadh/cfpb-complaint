1. Project Objective
This project is an important part of your continuous assessment in the Data Mining course.
It represents 20% of your Data Mining grade.
The purpose of this project is to help you apply the concepts and methods studied in
class to a real dataset. You are expected to conduct a clear and structured data mining
analysis and to explain your results in a rigorous but accessible way.
Your work should show that you are able to:
• understand a dataset and its context,
• define a relevant analytical objective,
• prepare and clean the data,
• apply appropriate data mining techniques,
• interpret the results correctly,
• and communicate your findings clearly.

2. Work Format
The project may be completed:
• in a group of three students.
For group work, both students must contribute actively to the analysis, the writing of the
report, and the presentation.
3. Expected Focus
This project must focus mainly on data mining, not only on simple descriptive statistics.
Depending on your dataset, your project may aim to:
• predict a variable,
• classify observations,
• group observations into clusters,
• detect patterns or anomalies,
• reduce dimensionality,
• or extract useful insights from the data.
You are not expected to use all possible methods. You should choose the methods that are
the most relevant for your dataset and your objective.
4. Software and Submission Format
You may complete the project using either:
• Python or
• R.

5.1. Title Page
Include:
• the title of your project,
• your name(s),
• the course title,
• and the video presentation link.
5.2. Dataset Description
Present the dataset clearly by answering questions such as:
• What is the general context of the data?
• What is the source of the dataset?
• Who collected the data, and when?
• What does one observation represent?
• What are the main variables?
5.3. Problem Statement
Clearly explain:
• the problem you want to study,
• the objective of your analysis,
• the main research question(s),
• and the target variable, if relevant.
5.4. Data Preparation
Explain the preprocessing steps that you performed, for example:
• handling missing values,
• detecting and treating outliers,
• transforming variables,
• encoding categorical variables,

• selecting useful variables.

5.5. Methods and Analysis
Present the methods that you used and explain why they are appropriate.
