1 COURSE OVERVIEW
This course is designed to provide TBS students with a detailed introduction to the Machine Learning

field. It covers fundamental concepts and tools of computational data science, including pattern clas-
sification, prediction, visualization, and neural networks. The course will give the student the basic

ideas and intuition behind Machine Learning methods as well as a bit more formal understanding of
how, why, and when they work.
Prerequisites: Basic knowledge of algebra, statistics, and programming with Python or R.
2 COURSE OBJECTIVES AND LEARNING OUTCOMES
By the end of the module, students should be able to:
1. Visualize and identify significant characteristics of data sets that may influence the choice of the
learning algorithm.
2. Understand a wide variety of learning algorithms.
3. Implement the training, testing, and validation phases of learning algorithms.
4. Find important features in the data and make feature selection.
5. Identify the risks of overfitting and underfitting.
6. Understand how to evaluate models generated from data.
7. Use suitable algorithms to solve real-life problems.
8. Identify the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning.
9. Understand how the boosting technique could improve the model performance.
10. Use the specialized R/ Python libraries to perform any of the algorithms covered by the course.
11. Technology adaptability: Get the ability to understand, adopt, and utilize new technologies to
remain competitive in the field. (This is the most important)
